# Ftira Bread

Traditional Maltese flatbread, similar to a focaccia, often enjoyed with tomato paste, olives, capers, or cheese.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 500 g strong white flour
- [ ] 1 tsp salt
- [ ] 1 tsp sugar
- [ ] 7 g instant dry yeast (1 packet)
- [ ] 300 ml warm water
- [ ] 2 tbsp olive oil (plus extra for drizzling)
- [ ] Optional toppings: sliced tomatoes, olives, capers, grated cheese, fresh herbs

## Steps

1. **Mix dry ingredients** – In a large bowl combine flour, salt, sugar, and yeast.
2. **Add liquids** – Make a well in the centre, pour in warm water and olive oil, then mix until a shaggy dough forms.
3. **Knead** – Turn onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8‑10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
4. **First rise** – Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a clean cloth and let it rise in a warm place for 1‑1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
5. **Shape** – Punch down the dough, divide into 2‑3 portions, and stretch each into a round or oval flatbread about 1 cm thick.
6. **Second rise** – Rest the shaped dough for 15‑20 minutes.
7. **Bake** – Pre‑heat the oven to 220 °C (425 °F). Place the ftira on a parchment‑lined tray, drizzle with olive oil and add desired toppings.
8. **Cook** – Bake for 12‑15 minutes, until golden on the edges and the toppings are cooked through.
9. **Serve** – Serve warm, split open and spread with tomato paste, olives, or cheese as desired.
